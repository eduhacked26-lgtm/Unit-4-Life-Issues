📚 Grammar Rules to Revise
  • -ure nouns: fail→failure, please→pleasure, expose→exposure.
  • -ic adjectives: atmosphere→atmospheric, economy→economic, science→scientific.
  • Adverbs of manner (-ly): swift→swiftly, rapid→rapidly, immediate→immediately.
  • Past simple passive: was/were + past participle.
  • un- + -ed adjective: prepared→unprepared, expected→unexpected.
  • Gerund after prepositions: for/of/about/without + verb-ing.

The (credible)  of news sources has become a central concern for citizens, educators, and governments in an era of widespread digital misinformation. The role of the (medium)  in shaping public perception of environmental and disaster events is both significant and complex, requiring constant editorial accountability and ethical reflection. A well-informed public is the best defence against misinformation, and that can only be achieved through investment in quality journalism, media literacy education, and independent regulation.
