Healthy Eating & Diet

/3

Put the words in brackets in the correct form/tense. There are two extra items.

Eating well is one of the most powerful things people can do to protect their long-term health. Doctors and (diet)  specialists agree that a balanced diet provides the body with the nutrients it needs to function (proper) . A diet rich in (nutrition)  foods such as fruit, vegetables, and whole grains helps prevent serious illness and maintain a healthy weight. Sadly, many people (give)  up good eating habits when work pressures made it difficult to cook at home regularly. Smoking remains one of the most (health)  choices a person can make, causing serious damage to the lungs and cardiovascular system. Medical (authority)  strongly advise those who wish to quit smoking to seek professional support rather than attempting to do so alone. In short, the choices people make every day about food and lifestyle have a direct and lasting impact on their overall wellbeing.

📚 Grammar Rules to Revise
  • Person nouns: diet→dietitian, physics→physician, music→musician.
  • Adverb -ly: proper→properly, clear→clearly, significant→significantly.
  • -ious adjectives: nutrition→nutritious, ambition→ambitious, space→spacious.

  • Plural -ies: authority→authorities, city→cities, country→countries.

Smoking & Personal Responsibility

Put the words in brackets in the correct form/tense. There are two extra items.

Tobacco addiction is one of the most serious public health challenges facing governments across the world today. The (depend)  of millions of people on tobacco is driven partly by the highly addictive nature of nicotine and partly by deeply ingrained social habits. Medical research (confirm)  that nicotine is one of the hardest substances to give up, making professional support essential for most smokers who wish to quit. Health organisations argue that campaigns must be delivered in a (sense)  and culturally appropriate manner to be genuinely effective among young people. (Restrict)  tobacco advertising in public spaces has already produced measurable reductions in the number of young people who start smoking each year. People who begin smoking in adolescence are (weight)  more likely to develop serious lung conditions in later life than those who have never smoked. A significant obstacle to quitting is the widespread (believe)  that smoking relieves stress, when research shows it actually increases anxiety levels over time. Tackling the tobacco epidemic will require sustained political will, strong legislation, and genuine investment in public health education at every level of society.
