The Workplace & Effective Management

/3

Put the words in brackets in the correct form/tense. There are two extra items.

The quality of management within an organisation has a direct and measurable impact on the wellbeing, motivation, and performance of every person who works there. Effective (manage)  is not simply about issuing instructions — it is fundamentally about building genuine trust and creating a culture where every employee feels (value) . Research (consistent)  shows that organisations with high staff engagement report significantly lower absenteeism, stronger performance, and much higher rates of long-term retention. Over the past decade, the understanding of good leadership (change)  profoundly, shifting away from traditional top-down authority towards more collaborative and empathetic approaches. When workers feel that their abilities are routinely (use) , many disengage from their roles and perform well below their true potential, causing harm to team morale and overall productivity. Good leaders understand that investing in their (employ)  through personalised training, regular feedback, and genuine recognition is one of the most cost-effective strategies available. In the end, the most successful organisations are those that treat their people not as resources to be managed but as valued partners whose contribution genuinely matters.

📚 Grammar Rules to Revise
  • -ment nouns: manage→management, achieve→achievement, develop→development.
  • -ed adjective (state): feel valued/motivated/respected — past participle after feel/seem.
  • Adverb position: Subject + adverb + verb: Research consistently shows.
  • Present perfect: has/have + past participle. Use with: over the past decade/year.
  • Prefix under-: use→underused, value→undervalued, pay→underpaid.
  • -er vs -ee: employer (acts) / employee (receives action).

Productivity, Wellbeing & Staff Retention

/3

Put the words in brackets in the correct form/tense. There are two extra items.

Understanding what motivates people at work is one of the most important and most challenging tasks that any manager or organisation must face. The measurable link between staff wellbeing and organisational (productive)  has been confirmed by dozens of independent studies conducted across a wide range of industries. Companies that invest in creating a genuinely supportive and (include)  working environment consistently outperform those that focus exclusively on short-term financial targets. A landmark study demonstrated (statistic)  that employees who felt respected by their managers were forty per cent more likely to remain with their organisation than those who did not. When a major company first (implement)  a four-day working week, staff reported significantly higher levels of energy, creativity, and job satisfaction across every department. Staff who feel (disengage)  from the organisation’s goals — or who believe their contributions are systematically ignored — are far more likely to seek employment elsewhere. A culture of open communication and genuine recognition typically produces (high)  levels of commitment and innovation than one built on fear, hierarchy, and micromanagement. Organisations that invest in their people not only retain talent more effectively but also build a reputation that attracts the best candidates from the wider job market.
